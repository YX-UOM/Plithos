## News Highlights

### SFDR 2.0: A Game-Changer for Real Estate Funds

The European Commission's [November 2025 proposal to overhaul SFDR](https://realassets.ipe.com/news/eu-confirms-overhaul-of-sfdr-rules-with-implications-for-real-estate-funds/10133819.article) represents the most significant regulatory shift since the original framework launched. Key changes include:

- **Abolition of Article 8/9 labels** - The often-confusing classification system is being replaced with clearer categories
- **Three new categories**: Transition (for assets undergoing decarbonization), Sustainable, and ESG Basics
- **Simplified disclosures** - Pre-contractual templates capped at two pages
- **70% investment thresholds** for each category

The new **Transition category is particularly significant for real estate**, recognizing that buildings are long-term assets requiring retrofit rather than replacement. This provides a regulatory pathway for brown-to-green strategies.

### Fidelity Raises $330M+ for Climate Real Estate Fund

[Fidelity International announced](https://esgnews.com/category/real-estate/) successful fundraising of over $330 million for a climate-focused real estate fund, demonstrating continued institutional appetite for sustainable property investments despite broader market uncertainty.

## Regulatory Updates

### EU: SFDR 2.0 and CSRD Changes

**SFDR 2.0 Timeline:**
- Proposal published: November 20, 2025
- Expected transition period: 12 months for existing funds to migrate
- [New ESA clarifications](https://www.regulatoryandcompliance.com/2025/08/new-sfdr-clarifications-published-by-the-european-supervisory-authorities/) on "energy consumption intensity" metrics for real estate assets (August 2025)

**CSRD Omnibus Proposal (February 2025):**
- Proposed threshold increase to 1,000+ employees and €450M turnover
- Aims to reduce administrative burden while maintaining transparency
- Sector-specific standards for real estate not yet in preparation

### UK: MEES and EPC Reform

**Current Requirements:**
- Minimum EPC rating of E required for all let commercial properties
- **April 2025**: All let properties must have valid EPC certificates

**Proposed Timeline:**
- **2027**: EPC C minimum for non-domestic rented buildings
- **2030**: EPC B minimum target
- Penalties: Up to £150,000 for non-compliance

**EPC Reform Consultation:**
- Launched December 2024, closing February 26, 2025
- Seeks to enhance accuracy and applicability of EPCs
- [London's response](https://www.london.gov.uk/sites/default/files/mol_response_commercial_mees_final.pdf) advocates for stronger standards

### US: Local Law 97 Enforcement Begins

[New York's Local Law 97](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/real-estate/our-insights/climate-risk-and-the-opportunity-for-real-estate) is now enforcing penalties for 2024 emissions:
- Estimated 5,300 of 34,000 in-scope buildings non-compliant
- Penalties: $268 per metric ton of CO₂e over the limit
- Caps tighten further in 2030
